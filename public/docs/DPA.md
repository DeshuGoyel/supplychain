# Data Processing Agreement (DPA)

**Effective Date:** January 12, 2025  
**Last Updated:** January 12, 2025

## 1. Introduction

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") forms part of the Terms of Service between Supply Chain AI ("Processor" or "we") and the customer ("Controller" or "you") who uses our Supply Chain AI Control Assistant platform.

## 2. Definitions

For the purposes of this DPA:
- **"Controller"** means the entity that determines the purposes and means of processing Personal Data
- **"Processor"** means the entity that processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller
- **"Data Subject"** means the individual to whom Personal Data relates
- **"Personal Data"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable individual
- **"Processing"** means any operation performed on Personal Data

## 3. Scope and Applicability

This DPA applies when we act as a Processor of Personal Data for you as the Controller. This DPA does not apply when we act as a Controller of Personal Data (e.g., for our own business operations).

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities

### 4.1 Controller Responsibilities
As the Controller, you agree to:
- Provide clear instructions for data processing
- Ensure you have the legal right to transfer Personal Data to us
- Ensure the data processing complies with applicable laws
- Obtain any necessary consents from Data Subjects
- Maintain appropriate documentation of processing activities

### 4.2 Processor Responsibilities
As the Processor, we agree to:
- Process Personal Data only on documented instructions from you
- Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure data security
- Ensure that personnel authorized to process Personal Data are bound by confidentiality obligations
- Assist you in responding to Data Subject requests and security incidents
- Delete or return Personal Data upon termination of the agreement

## 5. Personal Data Processing

### 5.1 Types of Personal Data
We may process the following types of Personal Data on your behalf:
- **Identity Data:** Names, email addresses, job titles, contact information
- **Business Data:** Company information, supplier details, inventory data, order information
- **Usage Data:** Platform usage statistics, login information, IP addresses
- **Financial Data:** Billing information, payment details (processed via Stripe)

### 5.2 Categories of Data Subjects
- Your employees and users of the platform
- Your customers or clients (if their data is processed through the platform)
- Your suppliers and business partners
- Other individuals whose data you provide to us

### 5.3 Processing Purposes
We process Personal Data solely for the following purposes:
- Providing the supply chain management platform
- Processing transactions and generating invoices
- Providing customer support and technical assistance
- Complying with legal obligations
- Improving our services through analytics

## 6. Technical and Organizational Measures

### 6.1 Security Measures
We implement the following technical and organizational measures:

**Access Controls:**
- Role-based access controls for all personnel
- Multi-factor authentication for administrative access
- Regular access reviews and updates
- Principle of least privilege

**Data Encryption:**
- Encryption in transit using TLS 1.3
- Encryption at rest using AES-256
- Key management using secure key storage

**Network Security:**
- Network segmentation and firewalls
- Intrusion detection and prevention systems
- Regular security assessments and penetration testing
- DDoS protection

**Data Protection:**
- Regular data backups with encryption
- Secure data disposal procedures
- Data loss prevention tools
- Monitoring and logging of data access

**Incident Response:**
- 24/7 security monitoring
- Incident response procedures
- Breach notification procedures
- Regular security training for personnel

### 6.2 Physical Security
- Secure data center facilities
- 24/7 monitoring and access controls
- Environmental controls (fire suppression, climate control)
- Secure disposal of hardware

## 7. International Data Transfers

### 7.1 Transfer Mechanisms
When transferring Personal Data outside the European Economic Area (EEA), we implement appropriate safeguards:
- **Adequacy Decisions:** Transfer to countries with adequate protection levels
- **Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs):** EU Commission-approved contractual clauses
- **Binding Corporate Rules:** For intra-group transfers
- **Certification Schemes:** Participating in recognized certification programs

### 7.2 Transfer Safeguards
- Risk assessments for international transfers
- Additional technical measures for sensitive data
- Regular review and updating of transfer mechanisms
- Documentation of transfer decisions and safeguards

## 8. Sub-Processors

### 8.1 Authorized Sub-Processors
We may engage the following sub-processors to assist in providing our services:

| Sub-Processor | Service Provided | Location | Data Processed |
|---------------|-----------------|----------|----------------|
| AWS | Cloud hosting and data storage | Multiple regions | All data types |

### 8.2 Sub-Processor Obligations
All sub-processors are bound by equivalent data protection obligations and must:
- Implement appropriate technical and organizational measures
- Maintain confidentiality and security
- Process data only on documented instructions
- Provide adequate guarantees of compliance

### 8.3 Notification of Changes
We will notify you in advance of any changes to our sub-processor list, and you may object to such changes on reasonable grounds.

## 9. Data Subject Rights

### 9.1 Assistance with Requests
We assist you in responding to Data Subject requests by:
- Providing tools and functionality within the platform
- Responding to technical requests within our systems
- Providing information about data processing activities
- Supporting compliance verification activities

### 9.2 Rights Support Matrix

| Right | Controller Action | Processor Assistance |
|-------|------------------|---------------------|
| Access | Respond to request | Provide data export |
| Rectification | Update data | Modify data in systems |
| Erasure | Delete data | Remove from systems |
| Portability | Provide export | Generate data files |
| Restriction | Mark data | Apply processing limits |
| Objection | Assess request | Stop direct marketing |

## 10. Data Breach Response

### 10.1 Detection and Assessment
We maintain procedures to:
- Detect and assess potential data breaches
- Notify relevant stakeholders within required timeframes
- Document all incidents and responses
- Conduct post-incident reviews

### 10.2 Notification Procedures
In the event of a Personal Data breach affecting your data:
- We will notify you without undue delay
- Provide information about the breach including nature, categories, and approximate number of affected individuals
- Describe likely consequences of the breach
- Outline measures taken or proposed to address the breach

### 10.3 Cooperation
We will cooperate with you in:
- Meeting regulatory reporting requirements
- Conducting data protection impact assessments
- Implementing additional security measures
- Managing communications with affected individuals

## 11. Audits and Compliance

### 11.1 Audit Rights
You may audit our compliance with this DPA by:
- Conducting on-site audits (with reasonable notice)
- Reviewing security certifications and reports
- Requesting information about data processing activities
- Using third-party audit services

### 11.2 Certifications and Standards
We maintain the following certifications and standards:
- SOC 2 Type II compliance
- ISO 27001 information security management
- GDPR compliance programs
- Regular penetration testing and security assessments

### 11.3 Documentation
We maintain documentation including:
- Data processing records
- Security policies and procedures
- Incident response plans
- Training records and awareness programs

## 12. Data Deletion and Return

### 12.1 Deletion Procedures
Upon termination of services or upon your request, we will:
- Delete all Personal Data from our active systems
- Ensure secure deletion from backup systems within 30 days
- Provide certification of deletion upon request
- Remove data from all data centers and cloud storage

### 12.2 Data Return
We can return Personal Data to you by:
- Providing secure data exports in standard formats
- Supporting data migration to alternative systems
- Ensuring data integrity during return process
- Providing technical assistance for data import

## 13. Liability and Compensation

### 13.1 Processor Liability
Our liability for breaches of this DPA is limited to direct damages up to the amount paid for our services in the 12 months preceding the breach, except in cases of:
- Gross negligence or willful misconduct
- Death or personal injury
- Fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation

### 13.2 Controller Liability
You remain liable for ensuring that your processing of Personal Data complies with applicable laws and that you have obtained all necessary consents and authorizations.

## 14. Term and Termination

### 14.1 Duration
This DPA remains in effect for the duration of our service provision and for 7 years after termination to comply with legal obligations.

### 14.2 Survival
The following provisions survive termination:
- Data deletion and return obligations
- Confidentiality obligations
- Audit rights and cooperation requirements
- Limitation of liability provisions
